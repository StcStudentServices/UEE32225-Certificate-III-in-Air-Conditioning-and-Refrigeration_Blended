3. Course Overview

This qualification provides individuals with the skills and knowledge to select, install, commission, test, fault-find, repair, and maintain air conditioning and refrigeration systems and associated equipment in residential, commercial, and industrial settings.

It includes the relevant technical and regulatory competencies required for working safely with electrical and refrigeration systems, including compliance with industry standards and licensing requirements.

The skills and knowledge described in this qualification require a licence or permit to practice in the workplace where work is carried out on electrical installations operating at voltages greater than 50V alternating current (AC) or 120V direct current (DC), as well as for handling refrigerants in accordance with national regulations.

Competency development activities are subject to licensing and regulatory requirements. Where a licence or permit is not held, training must be undertaken under appropriate supervision and, in most cases, through a formal training arrangement such as an Australian Apprenticeship.

    12. Theory Assessment conditions:

  • Your screen must be shared at all times.
  • Your camera must remain on so the supervisor can see you throughout the test.
  • The camera must be positioned so the supervisor can clearly see your face, hands, and workspace at all times.
  • Your microphone must remain on so the supervisor can hear if you speak.
  • You must be in a quiet room by yourself during the assessment. No other person is allowed in the room.
  • No notes, phones, or other materials may be used unless authorised by the trainer. A calculator is permitted.
  • At the beginning of the session, you may be asked to briefly show your surroundings using your camera.
  • Suspicious activity: Our LMS has a “Suspicious Activity” feature. If you open another tab or access unauthorised materials, the system will detect it, and the assessment may be stopped and marked as not satisfactory. This will count as an attempt, and you will need to reschedule.
  • Students are allowed two attempts per assessment (one resit). Additional attempts may incur fees. Please refer to your Letter of Offer and Student Handbook for details.

    • Failure to follow these conditions may result in the assessment being stopped, rescheduled, or marked as invalid.

    The End of Qualification Documentation is the final and essential stage of your qualification process.

    At this stage, you are required to submit a complete set of documents to verify your work experience, licensing status, and compliance with regulatory requirements.

    This documentation allows us to assess your eligibility for completion and certification.

    Please ensure that all forms are completed accurately and that all supporting evidence is current and valid

    All submitted documentation must be current and validated within the last three (3) months. Documents older than this period may not be accepted and may require re-issue or re-verification.
